# RB 304

Lifeboat House, The Quay

## Parish
Port St Mary

## Registration date
07/07/2021

## Extracted details
* Construction: 1896 – 1898
* Reasons for registration:
  - Late 19th century lifeboat house still in its original use and retaining original historic detailing.
  - Building illustrates the crucial role played by the RNLI in saving lives at sea since the 19th century.
  - William Hillary, founder of the RNLI, was from the Isle of Man and the island’s lifeboat stations are part of his legacy.

Register entry description 
History 
The Lifeboat Station was built between 1896 and 1898. Whilst the Station’s Trent class all-
weather lifeboat is stored in the harbour itself, the building remains in use to store the inshore 
lifeboat, crew facilities and as an operational base. 
Details 
MATERIALS: 
Stone and render, under slate roof 
PLAN: 
Simple rectangular plan 
EXTERIOR: 
Lime Street elevation,  gable end dressed coursed stone with in-filled boat entrance opening 
with render finish and door and window, round headed window above, decorative timber 

 
bargeboard and finial to verge.  
Southern elevation dressed coursed stone with three centrally placed round headed windows, 
decorated timber scalloped eaves bracket with dormer window above to seaward end with 
same detailing as main roof. Slate roof with terracotta ridge tiles.  
Seaward elevation, gable end dressed coursed stone, central large boat opening with pair of 
timber doors projecting forwards with slate monopitch roof, round headed window above with 
decorative timber bargeboard and finial to verge. 
 
INTERIOR: 
Modern mezzanine inserted over part of the interior. 
 
 
Reason for Registration Decision 
 
Lifeboat House, Port St Mary, is recommended for entry into the Protected Buildings Register 
for the following reasons: 
●ARCHITECTURAL INTEREST:  
Late 19th century lifeboat house still in its original use and retaining original historic detailing.

Building illustrates the crucial role played by the RNLI in saving lives at sea since the 19th 
century. 
●CLOSE HISTORICAL ASSOCIATION: William Hillary, founder of the RNLI, was from the Isle of
