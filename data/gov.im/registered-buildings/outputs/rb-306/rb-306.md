# RB 306

Former Lifeboat House, Breakwater Road

## Parish
Port Erin

## Registration date
07/07/2021

## Extracted details
* Construction: 1884 – 1925
* Reasons for registration:
  - Example of late-19th century lifeboat house with interesting architectural features.
  - Building illustrates the crucial role played by the RNLI in saving lives at sea since the 19th century.
  - William Hillary, founder of the RNLI, was from the Isle of Man and the island’s lifeboat stations are part of his legacy.

Register entry description 
History 
The building was constructed in 1884, and was Port Erin’s first lifeboat station as the village 
developed from a small fishing/mining community to a holiday resort. 
The building was replaced by a new lifeboat house in 1925. 
Details 
MATERIALS: 
Manx stone, Peel sandstone, slate roof and timber windows and doors 
PLAN: 
Rectangular plan under pitched roof 
EXTERIOR: 
South-Eastern gable Manx stone with large central door opening with timber door and 
louvered timber opening above. Quoins, kneelers, coping stones to parapeted verge and finial 

 
all in dressed Peel sandstone.  
North-Eastern elevation coursed Manx stone with Peel sandstone banding above window and 
door openings all with sandstone surrounds. Projecting base plinth with Peel sandstone 
banding course. 
North-Western gable, coursed stone with timber bargeboard central louvered opening with 
paired timber casement windows below, all with dressed Peel sandstone surrounds. Blocked 
arched doorway below again with Peel sandstone surround.  
 
INTERIOR: 
Originally two cells with larger boat house cell now subdivided to house public toilets.
