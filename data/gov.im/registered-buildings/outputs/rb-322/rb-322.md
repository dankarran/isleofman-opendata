Register entry description 
 
History 
 
The memorial was unveiled on 29 May 2012 by Captain Jack Ronan. Also present was His 
Excellency the Lieutenant Governor Adam Wood; Chairman of Port St Mary Commissioners, 
Bernadette McCabe; Director of Isle of Man Steam Packet Company, Kit Pemberton and four 
representatives from the French military who were closely involved in the operation to lift the 
anchor from the seabed at Dunkirk in 2010. 
 
The memorial commemorates those that lost their lives when Twin-screw Steamship (TSS) 
Mona’s Queen (3), together with King Orry (3) and Fenella (2), were lost during the 
evacuation of Dunkirk in May 1940. 
 
The memorial was dedicated by the Rev. Andrew Brown, Archdeacon of the Isle of Man.  
 
The memorial was designed by the local War Memorial Committee with final design by Patrick 
Collett. The memorial was built by R.G.W. Builders of Lezayre, Isle of Man.

Details 
 
FORM and MATERIALS: The memorial is circular in plan, with the restored anchor set in the 
centre on a raised base section. The anchor points in the direction of Dunkirk. A stone wall 
with grass bank behind includes tablets listing the names of those that were lost. Fixed seating 
in front of the wall and four flag poles behind complete the memorial. 
 
Subsidiary Features: The anchor in the centre of the memorial is from TSS Mona’s Queen (3), 
recovered from the sea bed off Dunkirk and restored by Cammell Laird in Birkenhead. 
 
 
Reason for Registration Decision 
 
Mona's Queen (Dunkirk) Memorial is recommended for entry into the Protected Buildings 
Register for the following reasons: 
 
● HISTORIC INTEREST: 
• 
The Mona’s Queen Memorial commemorates those that lost their lives when three IOM 
Steam Packet vessels were lost during the evacuation of Dunkirk in 1940. The 
memorial includes the anchor from TTS Mona’s Queen (3).
