Register entry description 
History 
The lighthouse was built to the designs of Robert Stevenson in 1818, at the same time as the 
nearby Upper Lighthouse, to warn maritime traffic of the Chicken Rock south of the Calf of 
Man. 
When the lighthouse on the Chicken Rock became operational, the light ceased operation. 
Details 
MATERIALS: Stone walls, parapets and chimney stacks. Lantern tower roof, lantern window 
framing and terrace guarding in metal. 
PLAN: Two storey rectangular accommodation block with symmetrical single storey projections 
off each gable. A cylindrical tower to the south of the accommodation block, approximately 
12m high, houses the lantern room itself. The tower is connected to the accommodation block 
via a single storey link section. 

 
 
EXTERIOR: Horizontally laid stone generally, with the opening surrounds, string course, 
quoins, parapet walls and gable chimney stacks in dressed stone. The cylindrical tower tapers 
slightly, with a 360 degree terrace. The lantern room features 360 degree glazing in its upper 
sections, with a metal roof above. 
 
INTERIOR: Not inspected.
