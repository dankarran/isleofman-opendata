Reasons for Registration 
Castle Rushen, Castletown, is included in the Protected Buildings Register for the following reasons: 
ARCHITECTURAL INTEREST:  
• 
With elements dating from 1200AD onwards, the Castle complex is a nationally important 
example of its building type, as well as being of importance in its architectural design, decoration 
and craftsmanship. 
HISTORIC INTEREST:  
• 
With elements dating from 1200AD, the site has been used as a royal residence, a mint, a 
prison and a courthouse in addition to its initial purpose as a military fortress. The complex 
illustrates importance aspects of the nation’s social and military history, and has close historic 
associations with the Stanley Family (Earls of Derby), and numerous other nationally important 
people including Bishop Wilson and Illiam Dhone (William Christian).
