Register entry description 
 
History 
 
The K6 (kiosk number six) was designed by Giles Gilbert Scott to commemorate the Silver 
Jubilee of George V. It was consequently sometimes known as the "Jubilee" kiosk. It went into 
production in 1936.  
 
Details 
 
FORM and MATERIALS: Four-sided rectangular box with a domed roof. Painted cast iron 
sections bolted together, standing on a concrete base. Painted timber door. Door and side 
panels are glazed with eight rows of three panes of glass, with a wide central pane.
