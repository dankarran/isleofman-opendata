Register entry description 
 
History 
 
The tower was constructed in 1871, funded by public subscription as a memorial to William 
Milner. 
 
Details 
 
MATERIALS: Local stone (slate), with sand cement render elements. 
 
PLAN: Cylindrical tower with a rectangular section at side. Spiral stair in tower, with single side 
room at ground and first floor, and observation area at top. The building’s rectangular section 
has a small cylindrical tower at each corner. 
 
EXTERIOR: Horizontally laid local stone (slate), with rendered plinth, string course, quoin 
details and surrounds to openings. Above the entrance to the tower there is a dedication 
stone. The observation area has a parapet wall with a sand/cement capping. 

 
 
INTERIOR: Stone spiral staircase. Metal gated access to side rooms at ground and first floor. 
 
 
Reason for Registration Decision 
 
Milner’s Tower is recommended for entry into the Protected Buildings Register for the 
following reasons: 
●ARCHITECTURAL INTEREST:  
19th century memorial tower in a distinctive form and style. 
●HISTORIC INTEREST:  
Notable structure associated with Port Erin Bay’s development dedicated to important local 
figure. 
●CLOSE HISTORICAL ASSOCIATION:  
Commemorative tower to William Milner, notable industrialist and benefactor.
