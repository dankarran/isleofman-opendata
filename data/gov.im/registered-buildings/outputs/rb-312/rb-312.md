Register entry description 
 
History 
 
Erected in 1858.  
 
Erected in memory of Brigadier-General Thomas Leigh Goldie, Lieutenant Colonel of H.M. 57th. 
Regiment, who commanded a Brigade of the British Army in the Crimean War, and fell in the 
Battle of Inkerman, Nov. 5th 1854. 
 
The memorial’s original form was that of an Obelisk with the captured gun placed in front. The 
Obelisk was damaged by a falling tree and has since been reconstructed. Sections of the 
Obelisk remain within the Nunnery grounds.

Details 
 
FORM and MATERIALS: tiered plinth which formed part of the original larger obelisk, with a 
gun set within a concrete mounting at the top of the remaining plinth. 
 
Subsidiary Features: The gun forming part of the memorial is Russian, captured during the 
Battle of Inkerman.
