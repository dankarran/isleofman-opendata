# RB 307

Harbourmaster's Office and Coal Shed

## Parish
Port Erin

## Registration date
07/07/2021

## Extracted details
* Construction: 1863
* Reasons for registration:
  - Mid-19th century maritime building retaining its original form and external appearance.
  - Harbourmaster’s office that is a surviving structure from the short-lived breakwater project.
  - The building is the island’s earliest surviving engine shed, pre-dating the railways developed in the 1870s.

Register entry description 
History 
Constructed in 1863 as part of the outer breakwater project. The building was used as a 
Harbourmaster’s Office, Coal Store and Engine Shed for the locomotive used on the 
breakwater railway. 
Details 
MATERIALS:  
Stone and render with slate roofs with clay ridge tiles 
PLAN:  
Rectangular plan with hipped roof and annex of same form but smaller in scale. 
EXTERIOR: 
First bay harbour master’s office, central door flanked by tripartite timber sash windows all 
with smooth render surrounds.  Chimney stacks on ridge above central doorway and upon side 

 
hipped roof slope with pair of window openings below, smooth render surround one with 
timber tripartite sash one blind with rendered wall. 
Following bays, timber casement window and garage opening both with smooth render 
surrounds. 
Timber casement window with pair of arched doorways with timber doors, two further timber 
casement windows  followed by door opening with timber door and three further timber 
casement windows . All under continuous slate roof with clay ridge tiles and coursed Manx 
stone walls. 
Western gable end, coursed stone wall with arched opening with large timber doors.   
Annexed coal store same materials as principle building with  three centrally placed timber 
casement windows to  principle elevation, gable end also with arched opening and large 
timber doors. 
 
INTERIOR: 
Not inspected 
 
 
Reason for Registration Decision 
 
Harbourmaster Office and Coal Store, Port Erin, is recommended for entry into the Protected
