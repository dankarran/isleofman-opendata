Register entry description 
History 
The precise date of construction is unknown, although the lighthouse is not in place in 
photographs dated mid-1880s, but is present in photographs dated ‘circa 1900’, and is also 
noted on the 1921 map.  
When originally built, the lighthouse upper housing was supported on an open lattice 
structure. The concrete tower is shown on photographs dating from the 1930s. 
Details 
MATERIALS: Painted timber lantern room/light enclosure with a concrete tower below. Painted 
timber access door. 
PLAN: Octagonal tower, tapering to its top. Octagonal lantern room above. Seaward facing 
sections of lantern room have windows in their upper half, while there is a door in the 
landward face. Single entrance door on the tower’s eastern (landward) face, accessed by 
concrete steps. 

 
 
EXTERIOR: White painted tower and lantern room with a single red band approximately 1m 
below the lantern room base.  
 
INTERIOR: Undecorated interior giving maintenance access to lantern room. 
 
Subsidiary features: The continuous red light has a range of 5 nautical miles and works in 
unison with a light sited on the Upper Promenade to mark a safe route to incoming marine 
traffic.  
 
 
Reason for Registration Decision 
 
Range Front (Leading Light) Lighthouse, Port Erin, is recommended for entry into the 
Protected Buildings Register for the following reasons: 
●ARCHITECTURAL INTEREST:  
Beachside lighthouse of a small-scale unique construction. 
●HISTORIC INTEREST:
